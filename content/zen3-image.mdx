The text-to-image tier of the Zen3 line. Prompt in, picture out. The interesting structural choice here is upstream's: the text encoder is a full vision-language model (`Qwen2_5_VLForConditionalGeneration`) rather than a CLIP or T5 tower, feeding a `QwenImageTransformer2DModel` on a flow-matching Euler schedule. That pairing is a large part of why the family handles long, compositional prompts and rendered text as well as it does.

It ships in standard diffusers layout, so it loads with one call and no vendor SDK. If you are generating UI mockups, diagrams, or anything with legible words in the frame, start here rather than with a CLIP-conditioned model.

## Lineage

Repackaged from [Qwen/Qwen-Image](https://huggingface.co/Qwen/Qwen-Image) (apache-2.0, Alibaba Qwen). Not trained from scratch, and not a Hanzo fine-tune. ## Use it

```python
import torch
from diffusers import DiffusionPipeline

pipe = DiffusionPipeline.from_pretrained(
    "zenlm/zen3-image", torch_dtype=torch.bfloat16
).to("cuda")

image = pipe(
    prompt="A chalkboard in an empty lecture hall, 'orthogonal' written on it",
    num_inference_steps=50,
).images[0]
image.save("out.png")
```
