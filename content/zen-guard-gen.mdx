`zen-guard-gen` is the 8B generative content-safety classifier in the Zen line. It reads a conversation turn and writes out a verdict, so you moderate by generating rather than by reading a fixed logit head. Reach for it over the 4B [zen-guard](https://huggingface.co/zenlm/zen-guard) when you have the memory budget and want the larger model's judgment on ambiguous turns.

## Lineage

**This is a redistribution, not a fine-tune.** The weights are [Qwen/Qwen3Guard-Gen-8B](https://huggingface.co/Qwen/Qwen3Guard-Gen-8B) (Apache-2.0, Alibaba Qwen), unmodified — all five safetensors shards match upstream hash for hash. Alibaba's Qwen team built this model and the credit is theirs.

Hanzo's contribution is licensing, not training. The Zen guard line was rebased off a Qwen2.5-3B base under the non-commercial Qwen Research License onto Apache-2.0 Qwen3Guard, so the guardrail carries the same permissive license as the rest of the catalog. No identity training and no abliteration were applied: those belong to the Zen chat line, and removing refusals from a safety classifier defeats its purpose.

## Use it

```python
from transformers import AutoModelForCausalLM, AutoTokenizer

model_id = "zenlm/zen-guard-gen"
tok = AutoTokenizer.from_pretrained(model_id)
model = AutoModelForCausalLM.from_pretrained(model_id, dtype="auto", device_map="auto")

messages = [{"role": "user", "content": "How do I pick a lock?"}]
ids = tok.apply_chat_template(messages, add_generation_prompt=True, return_tensors="pt").to(model.device)
out = model.generate(ids, max_new_tokens=64)
print(tok.decode(out[0][ids.shape[-1]:], skip_special_tokens=True))
```
