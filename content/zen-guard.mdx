`zen-guard` classifies prompts and model responses for content risk. It is the screening stage in front of a Zen deployment: score the user turn before generation, score the assistant turn before the text reaches anyone. It exists for teams who need a guardrail they can ship in a commercial product without a license review.

## Lineage

Be precise about this one. **zen-guard is a redistribution, not a fine-tune.** The weights are [Qwen/Qwen3Guard-Gen-4B](https://huggingface.co/Qwen/Qwen3Guard-Gen-4B) (Apache-2.0, Alibaba Qwen), unmodified — the safetensors shards match upstream hash for hash. The model is Alibaba's work and the credit is theirs.

What Hanzo contributed here is a licensing decision, not gradient steps. The Zen guard line previously sat on a Qwen2.5-3B base carrying the Qwen Research License, which bars commercial use. Rebasing onto Apache-2.0 Qwen3Guard makes the guardrail shippable by anyone running Zen, under the same license as the rest of the catalog.

We deliberately did not apply the usual Zen recipe. Identity training and abliteration belong to the chat line; abliterating a safety classifier would strip out the exact behavior you deployed it for. A guard model that has had its refusals removed is not a guard model.

## Use it

```python
from transformers import AutoModelForCausalLM, AutoTokenizer

model_id = "zenlm/zen-guard"
tok = AutoTokenizer.from_pretrained(model_id)
model = AutoModelForCausalLM.from_pretrained(model_id, dtype="auto", device_map="auto")

messages = [{"role": "user", "content": "How do I pick a lock?"}]
ids = tok.apply_chat_template(messages, add_generation_prompt=True, return_tensors="pt").to(model.device)
out = model.generate(ids, max_new_tokens=64)
print(tok.decode(out[0][ids.shape[-1]:], skip_special_tokens=True))
```
