zen-vl-4b-instruct takes images and text and returns text: captioning, document and screenshot OCR, chart reading, visual question answering. At 4B it runs comfortably beside the rest of a stack on one card. Its `config.json` declares 36 text layers at hidden size 2560, a 24-layer vision encoder, and a 262,144-token position limit.

## Lineage

Fine-tuned from [Qwen/Qwen3-VL-4B-Instruct](https://huggingface.co/Qwen/Qwen3-VL-4B-Instruct) (Apache-2.0), trained and released by Alibaba's Qwen team. The base is their work and the credit is theirs. We redistribute under the same license: Apache-2.0 upstream, Apache-2.0 here.

This is the one checkpoint in the Zen vision drop that carries real weight changes, and the diff says exactly where they are. Compared to upstream tensor by tensor, the language tower moved: token embeddings, attention projections, and MLP projections all differ. The vision encoder and the layer norms are bit-identical to Qwen's release. That is the signature of a language-side fine-tune over a frozen visual encoder, and the repository ships the `training_args.bin` from the run next to the weights.

We are not going to name a data recipe we cannot show you. The exact training mixture for this checkpoint is being confirmed and will be documented here once it is. What is verifiable today is the base, the license, and which tensors moved.

## Use it

```python
from transformers import AutoProcessor, AutoModelForImageTextToText

model_id = "zenlm/zen-vl-4b-instruct"
processor = AutoProcessor.from_pretrained(model_id)
model = AutoModelForImageTextToText.from_pretrained(
    model_id, dtype="auto", device_map="auto"
)

messages = [{
    "role": "user",
    "content": [
        {"type": "image", "url": "https://example.com/receipt.jpg"},
        {"type": "text", "text": "What is the total on this receipt?"},
    ],
}]

inputs = processor.apply_chat_template(
    messages, add_generation_prompt=True, tokenize=True,
    return_dict=True, return_tensors="pt",
).to(model.device)

out = model.generate(**inputs, max_new_tokens=256)
print(processor.decode(out[0][inputs["input_ids"].shape[1]:], skip_special_tokens=True))
```
